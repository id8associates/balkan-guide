Bosnia & Herzegovina: The Perfect 7-Day Itinerary

May 1, 2026 · 10 min read

Bosnia & Herzegovina is one of the most underrated destinations in Europe. From the Ottoman-era streets of Sarajevo to the iconic Stari Most bridge in Mostar, this country packs an incredible amount of history, nature, and hospitality into a small area. Here's the perfect 7-day itinerary.

Best time to visit: May-September. Spring for green landscapes and fewer crowds; summer for festivals and swimming at Kravice.

Day 1: Arrive in Sarajevo

Fly into Sarajevo International Airport (SJJ). Take a taxi or airport bus to the city center (€10 taxi, €3 bus). Check into your accommodation near Baščaršija, the old town.

Afternoon: Walk Baščaršija — the heart of Ottoman Sarajevo. See the Sebilj fountain, explore the copper alley (Kazandžiluk), and visit Gazi Husrev-beg Mosque.

Evening: Dinner at a traditional restaurant in the old town. Try ćevapi at Željo or Petica — a Sarajevo institution.

Accommodation: Stay near Baščaršija for easy walking access.

Day 2: Sarajevo — History & Culture

Morning: Visit the Tunnel of Hope (Sarajevo War Tunnel) — a sobering but essential experience. Then take the cable car to Trebević Mountain for panoramic views of the city.

Afternoon: Explore the Latin Bridge (where WWI started with the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand), the City Hall (Vijećnica), and the Museum of Crimes Against Humanity.

Evening: Walk up to the Yellow Fortress (Žuta Tabija) for sunset — the best view over Sarajevo's skyline.

Day 3: Sarajevo to Mostar via Konjic & Jablanica

Pick up a rental car or take the bus (2.5 hours). Stop at Konjic to see the Old Bridge and Ottoman houses. Continue to Jablanica for lunch — this town is famous for lamb roasted under a bell (sač).

Afternoon: Arrive in Mostar. Check in and walk the old town. Visit the iconic Stari Most (Old Bridge), a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Evening: Dinner at a restaurant overlooking the Neretva River. Watch the sunset from the bridge.

Day 4: Mostar & Kravice Waterfalls

Morning: Explore Mostar's old town — visit the Koski Mehmed Pasha Mosque for rooftop views, the Turkish House (Kajtaz House), and the old bazaar.

Afternoon: Drive 40 minutes to Kravice Waterfalls — a stunning 25-meter-high semicircular waterfall. Swim in the turquoise pools, relax on the grass, and have lunch at the waterfall restaurant. Entry fee: €5.

Evening: Back in Mostar, watch the traditional bridge divers leap from Stari Most (tips appreciated).

Day 5: Blagaj, Počitelj & Međugorje

Morning: Visit Blagaj Tekke — a 16th-century Dervish monastery built into a cliff at the source of the Buna River. Incredibly peaceful. Entry: €3.

Afternoon: Drive to Počitelj, a beautifully preserved Ottoman village on the Neretva River. Climb the clock tower for panoramic views. Then continue to Međugorje, a major Catholic pilgrimage site.

Evening: Return to Mostar for a final night. Try Burek for breakfast and local Herzegovinian wine for dinner.

Day 6: Mostar to Trebinje via the Adriatic Coast

Take a scenic drive through Herzegovina. Stop at Neum (Bosnia's only coastal town) for a swim break. Continue to Trebinje, a charming town known as the "City of Sun and Wine."

Afternoon: Explore Trebinje's old town, walk along the Trebišnjica River, and visit the Hercegovačka Gračanica monastery on the hill.

Evening: Wine tasting at a local winery — Trebinje is in the heart of Herzegovina's wine region.

Day 7: Sutjeska National Park to Sarajevo

Morning: Drive to Sutjeska National Park, home of Perućica — one of Europe's last primeval forests. Hike to Skakavac Waterfall (1.5 hours each way) or climb Maglić Mountain (Bosnia's highest peak at 2,386m).

Afternoon: Drive back to Sarajevo (2 hours) for your departure. If you have time, stop at Lukomir, Bosnia's highest village at 1,495m.

Evening: Final dinner in Sarajevo. Pick up some Bosnian coffee and baklava as souvenirs.

Budget Breakdown

  • Accommodation: €15-25/night (guesthouses) = €105-175
  • Food: €15-20/day = €105-140
  • Transport: Bus + rental car ~€80 total
  • Activities: Cable car, Kravice, museums ~€30
  • Total: ~€320-425 for 7 days
